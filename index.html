life insurance

Life insurance is a contract where you, the policyholder, pay a fee (a premium) to an insurance company, and in return, they promise to pay a lump sum of money (the death benefit) to your chosen beneficiaries after your death. This payout, also known as the death benefit, serves as a financial safety net to help your family cover expenses like living costs, mortgages, and children's education, ensuring their financial security in your absence.

Auto insurance

Auto insurance is a contract where you pay an insurer a premium for protection against financial losses from car accidents, theft, or other covered events, which can include damage to your own vehicle and liability for others' injuries or property. This coverage provides financial protection and peace of mind, acting as a safety net to prevent you from having to pay for all damages and injuries out-of-pocket.

Health insurance

Health insurance is a contract where you pay a regular premium to an insurance company, which in turn agrees to cover some or all of your medical costs in exchange for that payment. This coverage protects you from the high costs of medical services like hospitalizations, surgeries, and prescriptions, ensuring that unexpected illnesses or injuries don't financially devastate you.
